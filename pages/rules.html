Rules and Regulations

Game Rules

  • Throwing a bat, yelling at an umpire, using profanity and fighting with a player from another team will not be tolerated. It could result in ejection from or forfeit of the game.
  • No fast pitch. We want the games to be competitive, so pitching speed should be slow enough for people to hit, but not so slow that teams are pitching underhand. Curve balls, sidearm throwing and slides are allowed.
  • The number of players per team are as follows:
  • COMPETITIVE: 4 players in the field at once (3 position players and a pitcher)
  • FRIENDLY: 5 players in the field at once (4 position players and a catcher)
  • The number of innings will be as follows:
  • COMPETITIVE: 6 innings, 2 outs/inning
  • FRIENDLY: 5 innings, 3 outs/inning. After 6 pitched balls, the pitcher must pitch underhand.
  • Each team has unlimited fielding substitutions, but fielding changes can only be made before the inning. Once a team is out in the field, they are set until the next inning.
  • One pitching substitution can be made per inning, but the player being subbed in must already be in the field.
  • Once the batting order of a team is set, it cannot be changed. If your team bats out of order, it is considered an out.
  • Bunting is not allowed. The first time you attempt a bunt, you will get a warning. From then on, every time your team attempts to bunt, it will be considered an out.
  • Balls, strikes, fouls and outs
  • 3 strikes is an out.
  • There will be no walks.
  • Fouls are strikes unless a batter has two strikes. When a batter has two strikes and the batter hits a foul tip that hits the strike zone, that batter will be out.
  • A hit-by-pitch is considered a ball (no base is awarded). If a player intentionally leans into a pitch, it will be called a strike.
  • Players will bat until they strike out or put the ball into play.
  • Base runners must remain on base until the ball is hit. Stealing bases is not allowed.
  • Any ball that lands beyone the outfield wall/cones that is fair on the fly is considered a homerun. Robbing homeruns is allowed as long as the fielder has one foot in play or if the fielder is able to leap from the field and catch the ball in the air before landing.

  • All runners on a base are safe, unless there are two people on a base. If this is the case, the lead runner is out and the second runner is safe.
  • Pegging is allowed FOR COMPETITIVE PLAY ONLY (it will not be allowed in friendly games). (Note: pegging is when you throw the wiffle ball at a runner, and the runner that is hit is out and cannot advance.)

General Rules

  • Alcohol is permitted at the pavilion only. BYOB.
  • There are a limited number of grills available located in the pavilion area for use on first come first serve basis. Please bring your own cooking supplies and ingredients, as they will not be provided.
  • Bats and balls will be provided by the KG Scholarship Tournament Committee.
  • All players must play bare handed.
  • There will be an umpire at every game in the field. Umpires are in charge of keeping track of balls and strikes, calling fair and foul balls, safe and out calls, batter interferences and homeruns.
  • For strikes and balls a chair, provided by the KG Tournament Committee, will be used as the strike zone.
  • Each field will have the same size and color chair.
  • The back rise of the chair will be the strike zone. Any pitch that hits any part of the back rise is a strike. If the pitch does not come in contact directly with the back rise of the chair, it is called a ball. The pitch cannot bounce off of any other part of the chair and then hit back rise; that will be considered a ball.
  • Taunting is allowed, but physical contact and profanity will not be tolerated.
  • Throwing a bat, yelling at an umpire, using profanity and fighting with a player from another team will not be tolerated. It could result in ejection from or forfeit of the game.
